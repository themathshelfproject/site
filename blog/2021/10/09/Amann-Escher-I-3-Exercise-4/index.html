Problem

Let $f: X \to Y$ be a function. Show that the following are equivalent

(a) $f$ is injective

(b) $f^{-1}(f(A)) = A$, (where $A \subseteq X$)

(c) $f(A \cap B) = f(A) \cap f(B)$, (where $A, B \subseteq X$)

Solution

Lemma I: Let $A \subseteq X$. Then, $f^{-1}(f(A)) = A$ $\iff$ $f$ is injective

Proof:

We will first show that if $f^{-1}(f(A)) = A$, $f$ must be injective.
