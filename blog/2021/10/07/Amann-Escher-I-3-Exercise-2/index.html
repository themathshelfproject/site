Problem

Prove that the following hold for the set-valued functions induced from $f$

(i) $A \subseteq B \subseteq X \implies f(A) \subseteq f(B)$

(ii) $A_i \subseteq X$ where $i \in I$ (some index set) $\implies f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right) = \bigcup_i f(A_i)$

Solution

(i) Let $y$ be an arbitrary element of $f(A)$.

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} y \in f(A) &\implies \exists x \in A \text{ such that } f(x) = y &&\text{ (by defn. of a function image)} \\ &\implies x \in B &&\text{ (since $A \subseteq B$)} \\ &\implies y \in f(B) &&\text{ (by defn. of a function image)} \end{alignat} $$

Thus, $f(A) \subseteq f(B)$.


(ii) We first show that $f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right) \subseteq \bigcup_i f(A_i)$. For this, let $y \in f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right)$

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} y \in f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right) &\implies x \in \bigcup_i A_i \text{ such that } f(x) = y &&\text{ (by defn. of a function image)} \\ &\implies \exists j \in I \text{ such that } x \in A_j &&\text{ (since $A_j \subseteq \bigcup_i A_i$)} \\ &\implies y \in f(A_j) &&\text{ (since $f(x) = y$)} \\ &\implies y \in \bigcup_i f(A_i) &&\text{ (since $f(A_j) \subseteq \bigcup_i f(A_i)$)} \\ \end{alignat} $$

Thus, $f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right) \subseteq \bigcup_i f(A_i)$.

We now show the reverse, i.e. that $\bigcup_i f(A_i) \subseteq f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right)$. For this, let $y \in \bigcup_i f(A_i)$.

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} y \in \bigcup_i f(A_i) &\implies \exists j \in I \text{ such that } y \in f(A_j) &&\text{ (since $f(A_j) \subseteq \bigcup_i f(A_i)$)} \\ &\implies \exists x \in A_j \text{ such that } f(x) = y &&\text{ (by defn. of a function image)} \\ &\implies x \in \bigcup_i A_i &&\text{ (since $A_j \subseteq \bigcup_i A_i$)} \\ &\implies y \in f\left(\bigcup_i A_i \right) &&\text{ (since $f(x) = y$)} \\ \end{alignat} $$

Thus, $f\left(\bigcup_i A_i\right) \subseteq \bigcup_i f(A_i)$.

This, in conjunction with the forward case completes the proof.
