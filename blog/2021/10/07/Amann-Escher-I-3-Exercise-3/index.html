I.3 Exercise 3

Published

07 October 2021

Problem

Let $f: X \to Y$, $g: Y \to Z$ be functions. Show the following

(i) If $f, g$ are injective (surjective), so is $g \circ f$

(ii) $f$ is injective $\iff$ $\exists h: Y \to X$ such that $h \circ f = id_X$

(iii) $f$ is surjective $\iff$ $\exists h: Y \to X$ such that $f \circ h = id_Y$

Solution

(i) We will first show that if $f, g$ are injective, so is $g \circ f$. By the definition of composition, we know that $g \circ f: X \to Z$ is a well-defined map. Now, let $x_1, x_2$ be arbitrary elements of $X$ such that $(g \circ f)(x_1) = (g \circ f)(x_2)$. Then,

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} (g \circ f)(x_1) = (g \circ f)(x_2) &\implies g(f(x_1)) = g(f(x_2)) &&\text{ (by defn. of composition)} \\ &\implies f(x_1) = f(x_2) &&\text{ (since $g$ is injective)} \\ &\implies x_1 = x_2 &&\text{ (since $f$ is injective)} \end{alignat} $$

This completes the proof.

We will now show that if $f, g$ are surjective, so is $g \circ f$.

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} (g \circ f)(X) &= g(f(X)) &&\text{ (by defn. of composition)} \\ &= g(Y) &&\text{ (since $f(X) = Y$ by surjectivity of $f$)} \\ &= Z &&\text{ (since $g(Y) = Z$ by surjectivity of $g$)} \\ \end{alignat} $$

This completes the proof.


(ii) We will first prove, constructively, that $f$ is injective $\implies$ $\exists h: Y \to X$ such that $h \circ f = id_X$. Let $h: Y \to X$ be given by the rule below. Note that since $f$ may not necessarily be surjective, $h$ could be ill-defined if we only specified mappings for $y \in f(X)$.

$$ \begin{cases} y \mapsto x \text{ such that } f(x) = y & \text{ if } y \in f(X) \\ y \mapsto y & \text{ if } y \in Y - f(X) \end{cases} $$

Now, by the definition of composition, we know that $h \circ f: X \to X$ is a well-defined map. Further, $\forall x \in X$ we have, by function composition and the mapping rule for $h$,

$$ (h \circ f)(x) = h(f(x)) = x $$

Thus, $h \circ f = id_X$. This completes the proof of the forward case.

We will now show that if there exists $h: Y \to X$ such that $h \circ f = id_X$, $f$ is injective. Let $x_1, x_2$ be arbitrary elements of $X$ such that $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$. Then, we have

$$ \begin{alignat}{2} (h \circ f)(x_1) &= h(f(x_1)) &&\text{ (by defn. of composition)} \\ &= h(f(x_2)) &&\text{ (since $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$)} \\ &= x_1 &&\text{ (since $h \circ f = id_X$)} \\ &= x_2 &&\text{ (since $h \circ f = id_X$)} \\ \end{alignat} $$

Thus, $x_1 = x_2$ and $f$ is injective. This completes the reverse case and the proof.
